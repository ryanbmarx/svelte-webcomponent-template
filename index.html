The Social Mobility Engine

A FutureEd Policy Tool to Combat Poverty and Drive Opportunity

What if you—a governor, city leader or foundation executive—knew that spending $9,000 for a toddler to attend Head Start would increase their income by 7 percent when they graduated high school? What if you knew that an additional investment of $10,000 in affordable housing for that toddler’s family would boost the child’s salary at age 25 by 19 percent? And what if you could calculate those effects not just for a single child, but an entire community? With FutureEd’s Social Mobility Engine, you can do just that. The Mobility Engine is a dynamic, customizable tool that allows policymakers to model how investments in education, housing, healthcare and other sectors can work together to strengthen student and family outcomes over time, spurring social mobility. Based on national data.
